FIFA WORLD CUP KNOWLEDGE BASE SUMMARY
Curated file for RAG retrieval: winners, finals, scorelines, top scorers, major awards, records, milestones, and main tournament facts.

IMPORTANT DATA NOTES:
- This file is designed to improve retrieval for common FIFA World Cup questions.
- Official individual awards changed over time.
- Golden Ball was first officially awarded in 1982.
- Golden Boot / Golden Shoe was first officially awarded in 1982, although top scorers are known for earlier tournaments.
- Golden Glove / Yashin Award was first officially awarded in 1994.
- Best Young Player Award was first officially awarded in 2006.
- Top assists are not consistently official for every historical World Cup. For all-time assist records, see the records section.
- Team of the Tournament / All-Star Team has changed format over different editions, so it is not consistently listed tournament-by-tournament here.
- For exact match-by-match results beyond finals, add a structured CSV match-results dataset later.

GENERAL FIFA WORLD CUP OVERVIEW:
The FIFA World Cup is the main international association football tournament for senior men's national teams.
It is organized by FIFA and usually takes place every four years.
The first FIFA World Cup was held in 1930 in Uruguay.
The tournament was not held in 1942 and 1946 because of World War II.
Brazil has won the most FIFA World Cups with five titles.
Germany and Italy have each won four FIFA World Cups.
Argentina has won three FIFA World Cups.
Uruguay and France have each won two FIFA World Cups.
England and Spain have each won one FIFA World Cup.
Brazil is the only national team to have played in every FIFA World Cup tournament.
Miroslav Klose is the all-time top goalscorer in FIFA World Cup history with 16 goals.
Pelé is the only player to win three FIFA World Cups as a player.
Just Fontaine holds the record for most goals in a single World Cup tournament with 13 goals in 1958.
Oleg Salenko holds the record for most goals by one player in a single World Cup match with 5 goals for Russia against Cameroon in 1994.
Hakan Şükür scored the fastest goal in FIFA World Cup history after about 11 seconds for Turkey against South Korea in 2002.
Roger Milla is one of the oldest goalscorers in FIFA World Cup history, scoring for Cameroon in 1994 at age 42.
Essam El-Hadary became the oldest player to appear in a FIFA World Cup match in 2018 at age 45.
Lionel Messi holds the record for most FIFA World Cup appearances by a player, with 26 appearances.
Pelé is often credited as one of the all-time assist leaders in World Cup history, with sources varying between 8 and 10 assists.
Raymond Kopa is commonly cited for one of the highest assist totals in a single tournament, with 9 assists in 1958.
Lionel Messi assisted in five different FIFA World Cup tournaments: 2006, 2010, 2014, 2018, and 2022.

============================================================
AWARDS AND TERMS DICTIONARY
============================================================

Golden Boot:
The Golden Boot is awarded to the top goalscorer of the tournament.
Before 1982, the term Golden Boot was not officially used, but tournament top scorers are known historically.
The Golden Boot was previously called the Golden Shoe from 1982 to 2006.

Golden Ball:
The Golden Ball is awarded to the best player of the tournament.
It was first officially awarded in 1982.
Golden Ball means best player or player of the tournament.

Golden Glove:
The Golden Glove is awarded to the best goalkeeper of the tournament.
It was previously known as the Yashin Award from 1994 to 2006.
Golden Glove means best goalkeeper or goalkeeper of the tournament.

Best Young Player Award:
The Best Young Player Award recognizes the best young player of the tournament.
It was first officially awarded in 2006.

Fair Play Award:
The FIFA Fair Play Trophy recognizes the team with the best fair play record among eligible teams.

Team of the Tournament:
Team of the Tournament can refer to an official or selected best XI / All-Star Team / Dream Team depending on the edition.
Because the format changed over time, it should be answered only when the retrieved source clearly states the team.

Top assist maker:
Top assist maker means the player with the most assists.
Assist data is less consistently official in older World Cup editions.
If the retrieved context does not clearly mention assists for a specific tournament, the system should say that the assist detail is missing from the dataset.

Scoreline:
Scoreline means the final score of a match.
For World Cup finals, this file includes the final scoreline for every tournament from 1930 to 2022.
For 1950, there was no standard knockout final. The decisive match was Uruguay 2-1 Brazil.
